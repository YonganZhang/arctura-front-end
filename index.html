How a project flows

Six stages,
one living project.

Every stage reads from the same source of truth. Change one decision — a dimension, a material, a code, a budget — and every downstream deliverable regenerates. Nothing gets out of sync.

01 · Intake
The brief, in any form
A sentence, a sketch, a site photo, a CAD drawing, an existing IFC model, a reference image, or a Pinterest board — we take whatever the project already has.
text · sketch · CAD · IFC · photos · refs
02 · Scope
Program & constraints
Site, program, budget, local codes, accessibility, acoustic & thermal targets are locked as a single scope. Conflicts surface before design starts.
scope confirmed · open questions flagged
03 · Design
Plans & 3D model
Floorplans, sections, elevations, and a full 3D model. Exported in formats your engineers, contractors and BIM team already use.
drawings · sections · 3D · IFC4
04 · Visualize
Renders & mood
Hero, main zone, feature wall, bird's eye — consistent camera, lighting and material language across every revision.
consistent camera · consistent light · consistent material
05 · Quantify
Budget, energy, compliance
Priced BOQ in local currency, simulated energy performance & thermal comfort, plus a code-by-code compliance check against the jurisdiction.
BOQ · EUI · daylight · code check
06 · Package
Decks per audience
The same project, retold for each stakeholder — client, investor, contractor, engineer, marketing — with language and data weight tuned to the room.
decks per audience · one source of truth

核心科技 · Core technology

Four layers. One engine.

01
AI-driven
Generative and reasoning models tuned for architectural work — brief parsing, plan generation, material language, render control. Not a wrapper on a generalist model. A stack built for how rooms and buildings actually work.
02
Research roots
Graduate-level work in generative design and spatial intelligence. The pipeline reflects how a schooled architect thinks — not how a generalist model guesses.
03
Domain depth
Years of boutique practice layered into every default, every preset, every reference. The quiet things — what looks right, what breaks on site, what a contractor will flag — are already in the system.
04
Compounding corpus
Every project feeds a private library of drawings, materials, code mappings and precedents. Each engagement ships faster because the one before it left something behind.
